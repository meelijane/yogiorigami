developing the ashtanga primary series

Join Sandra Leong and Rosalyn Lim for a five-week introduction to Ashtanga. 

The Ashtanga Yoga method incorporates synchronising of breath with a progressive series of poses that build in strength and flexibility.

These classes are designed for students looking to discover Ashtanga Yoga and those who wish to refine their existing practice. We will guide you through the rudiments of the primary series and provide the foundations for building a stable, safe and strong practice which can be applied to all styles of yoga.

week 1: Ujjayi breath, downward facing dog and upward facing dog 

We start at the core of the practice– the breath. The breath technique employed in the Ashtanga tradition is Ujjayi breath, which translates into ‘victorious breath’. Learn and refine this breath technique, gain a better understanding of its many benefits and how it contributes to greater awareness and grounding in your practice.

week 2: Uddiyana Bandha,
Chaturanga Dandasana 

An integral part of the breathing system is Uddiyana Bandha and Mula Bandha. Uddiyana bandha refers to the lower abdominal lock. This week learn how it supports the breath, how it influences asanas from an anatomical and energetic perspective, and begin to incorporate it into your asana practice.

week 3: Mula Bandha
Developing the standing series

Following from the week before, Mula Bandha refers to the contraction of the pelvic floor muscles. This week we run briefly through an anatomy breakdown of the pelvic floor, and work on bringing attention to the centre of the perineum.

week 4: Drishti,
Urdhva Dharunasana

There are nine specific gazing directions that serve to develop concentration intention. Learn how incorporation of this is a step towards Dharana and ultimately Dhyana

week 5: Tristhana
Sirsasana

We combine all that we have learned by employing all three actions central to the Ashtanga Yoga method– asana, the breath and gazing points. Through this, we begin to work toward purifying the body, the nervous system and the mind.
