Refining the ashtanga primary series

Join Sandra Leong and Rosalyn Lim for a five-week course Ashtanga training course. 

This course is designed for students who have a base understanding of the Primary series and wish to deepen their knowledge of the practice. We will guide you through the finer points of the primary series; provide modifications for the more intermediate/advanced asanas, as well as exercises that will help you develop the awareness, engagement and strength to advance in your yoga practice.

week 1: Recap/Refine

Committing the sequence to memory is essential in the Ashtanga tradition. As you become familiar with the sequence, it allows you to focus less on what’s coming next, more on internal awareness. This week we take you through half primary as covered in the first course, with an emphasis on the 3 pillars that make up the core of the practice—the breath, the bandhas and the gaze..

week 2: Modifications

Yoga isn’t a one size fits all practice— the full expression of some asanas may not be available to some practitioners due to reasons such as old injuries, anatomical restrictions, the list goes on. This week we explore asana modifications that will enable practitioners to work with where their bodies are at and help them go deeper.

week 3: Transitions
(Jump Back/Jump Through)

These hallmarks of the Ashtanga Vinyasa traditional allow for breath effective transitions between each posture. This week we provide a step by step break down that will allow you to develop the strength and endurance to stay the course through this challenging movement.

week 4: Halfway mark of Primary
(Bhuja/Kurma/Supta)

The half primary sequence stops at Supta Kurmasana, which is the great gateway posture that forms the crescendo of the Primary series. Gateway poses mean exactly as it sounds; it stops a student from progressing to the next pose until enough exploration and depth has been invested. This week, in a highly individualised practice, we will stop here and explore these poses a little further and with detailed technical instruction.

week 5: Closing postures

The closing postures of Ashtanga yoga is a powerful and grounding way to end the practice. These specific poses restore the nervous system and guide the body back to balance. This week we explore each of these poses and give shoulder stand and head stand another closer look.
